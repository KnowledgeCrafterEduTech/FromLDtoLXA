# Learning Design Practice Lab — J&J Innovative Medicine
**Bloom's Taxonomy progression · 6-month readiness target**

Two roles, one shared floor. Learning Designer executes a blueprint someone else wrote. Learning Experience Architect (LXA) writes that blueprint. Every module below is tagged with the cognitive level it trains, per Bloom's Taxonomy: **Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create**.

---

## Track 1 — Learning Designer (Execution)

*Reports to the Lead Architect. Stays mostly in Apply/Analyze — execution role, intentionally lower ceiling than LXA.*

### Module 1 — Adult Learning Foundations
**Bloom level: Remember**
**Duty:** Strong grounding in instructional design principles and adult learning practices

**Scenario:** A junior SME drafts a module on a new pricing policy. It opens with: *"By the end of this module, you will recall the 6 tiers of the new pricing structure."* 14 slides of tier definitions follow, then a 20-question multiple choice exam.

**Model answer:** The objective itself is the problem — "recall" targets memorization, but a sales rep needs to apply the right tier to a real customer scenario under time pressure on a call. Rewrite the objective around application: "given a customer profile, select the correct pricing tier and justify it." That cascades through everything — the 14 definition slides become a quick-reference job aid, and the 20-question recall exam becomes 4-5 realistic scenario-based decisions.

---

### Module 2 — Direction → Detailed Assets
**Bloom level: Apply**
**Duty:** Translate high-level design direction into storyboards, scripts, activities, assessments

**Brief from the Lead Architect:** *"We need a 5-minute digital module teaching field reps how to respond when a healthcare provider asks an off-label usage question during a sales call. Tone: calm, compliant, confident. Must end with a knowledge check on the correct escalation path."*

**Task:** Build one storyboard frame (frame 3 of 5, the "decision point" frame):
- Screen description
- On-screen text
- Narration / voiceover script (word for word)
- Interaction

**Model answer:** Extract the performance objective hiding inside the direction — here, it's not "know the policy," it's "recognize the moment and escalate correctly under social pressure." Script the HCP's question with realistic phrasing, give the learner a moment of ambiguity before the interaction, and make at least one wrong answer tempting.

---

### Module 3 — Knowledge Checks That Work
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Create knowledge checks, performance-based activities, reinforcement mechanisms

**Case:** Question as drafted — *"True or False: Field reps must escalate any off-label question to Medical Affairs within 24 hours."* Objective: *"Recognize an off-label question in real time and redirect appropriately during the live interaction."*

**Quiz:** What's the core mismatch?
- ✅ It tests recall of a policy fact, but the objective requires real-time recognition and redirection — needs a performance-based scenario instead.

---

### Module 4 — Integrating Third-Party Content
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Identify, assess, adapt, and integrate existing content and third-party resources

**Scenario:** A vendor-produced 15-minute video on device sterilization exists from 2 years ago. Well-produced, but references an older device model, different branding, no captions.

**Build:**
1. Reuse, adapt, or rebuild? State your decision.
2. If adapting, what specifically changes?
3. How do you keep instructional coherence with the rest of the module?

---

### Module 5 — Blueprint to Aligned Output
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Work from solution blueprints and business requirements to produce aligned outputs

**Blueprint vs. draft:**
- Blueprint: Audience = experienced reps (2+ years), peer-to-peer tone
- Draft: Opens with basic definitions, reads like onboarding for new hires

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Cross-check stated audience against actual tone and assumed prior knowledge
- [ ] Flag content that re-teaches what the audience already knows
- [ ] Verify business requirements are reflected in the actual deliverable
- [ ] Check tone/voice matches what was specified
- [ ] Document deliberate deviations so they don't read as oversights

---

### Module 6 — Templates, Polish & Accessibility QA
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Apply templates, standards, and review feedback for accessible learning products

**Draft slide under review (Slide 7, dosing_guidelines.pptx):**
- Visual: Red text on light pink, no alt text on chart, 11px body
- Heading: "DOSING!!" all caps
- Check: True/False with no feedback shown

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Red-on-pink fails WCAG color contrast
- [ ] Missing alt text — inaccessible to screen reader users
- [ ] 11px body text below minimum readable size
- [ ] All-caps heading reads as shouting
- [ ] Knowledge check with no feedback wastes the learning moment

---

### Module 7 — Measurement & Continuous Improvement
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Embed measurement components and support continuous improvement

**Data pattern:** Completion 94%, quiz score 91% — but field coaching shows reps still mishandle the targeted scenario.

**Quiz:** Most likely design flaw?
- ✅ The knowledge check tests recall, not applied decision-making under pressure — redesign as scenario-based practice.

---

### Module 8 — Stakeholder Refinement Loop
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Collaborate with Lead Architect, SMEs, business partners; communicate design choices

**Three conflicting notes:**
- SME: "Needs 3 more slides of mechanism detail."
- Designer: "Already too text-heavy, cut don't add."
- Business partner: "Reps need this live, not by Friday."

**Model answer:** Go back to the learning objective rather than satisfying all three literally. If the rep needs to recognize when to redirect rather than recite mechanism detail, the SME's content becomes a linked job aid instead of more screens. For the deadline, split "must-have for launch" from "v1.1" and bring that as a proposal.

---

### Module 9 — Design Ops: Templates & Organization
**Bloom level: Apply**
**Duty:** Maintain templates, design documentation, and asset organization

**Situation:** Three modules into a 12-module compliance series. Define the naming/folder system before module 4.

**Build:**
1. File naming convention (one real filename)
2. Folder structure (top 3 levels)
3. What signal means this system needs to change?

---

### Module 10 — Final Boss: Tools & the Experience Gap
**Bloom level: Apply**
**Duty:** Authoring platforms, AI-assisted resources, 7+ years incl. Agency/Consulting/Life Sciences

**Real tasks:**
1. **Open Articulate Rise. Build the Module 2 storyboard frame as a real lesson block.** No reveal here — this is the actual task, 30-40 minutes. "I'm proficient with Rise" becomes true the moment you've built something.
2. **If asked about Storyline branching, and you haven't shipped one:** *"I'm hands-on in Rise, and building toward Storyline for branching logic specifically — the decision-tree thinking is exactly what I design when scripting a decision-point frame. I haven't shipped a Storyline branching scenario yet, but the design thinking transfers directly."*
3. **On the 7+ years / Agency/Consulting/Life Sciences gap:** *"My 20+ years are concentrated in education rather than those specific sectors — I want to be upfront. What I'd bring instead is real instructional design craft, which I can show you in a complete HDD I authored and built end-to-end."*

## Track 2 — Learning Experience Architect (Strategy)

*Senior individual contributor. Diagnoses the performance gap, authors the HDD, mentors designers, owns the stakeholder relationship.*

### Module 1 — Read an HDD Section by Section
**Bloom level: Remember**
**Duty:** Foundational fluency — know what each HDD section is doing

**Task:** Open your own HDD. For each of the 9 sections, write one sentence: what business question does this section answer, and who would push back if it were missing?

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Executive Summary — what question does it answer for a time-pressed VP
- [ ] Business Context & Performance Gap — what evidence trail it should show
- [ ] Solution Architecture — the instructional model and modality justification
- [ ] AI Integration & Responsible Use — the human/AI decision split
- [ ] Measurement & Evaluation — the named, real signal (not a vague promise)
- [ ] Accessibility & Governance — the named standard and concrete features
- [ ] Risks & Dependencies — at least one risk you'd be uncomfortable admitting

---

### Module 2 — Diagnose the Real Performance Gap
**Bloom level: Analyze**
**Duty:** Performance consulting — translate a vague request into the actual root cause

**Request:** *"New reps take 6 months to hit quota. We need better onboarding training."* Sales data: reps who hit quota fastest weren't the highest quiz scorers — they were paired with a mentor in week one.

**Model answer:** The real gap isn't content knowledge — quiz scores didn't predict success, mentorship pairing did. Name the gap as "lack of structured peer-modeled practice in week one," not "lack of training content." That reframes the entire solution architecture: mentorship pairing becomes the central intervention.

---

### Module 3 — Evaluate a Stakeholder's Counter-Argument
**Bloom level: Evaluate**
**Duty:** Defend a design decision with evidence, not authority

**Pushback:** *"I don't buy the mentorship theory — give every new rep a mentor and watch quota times barely move. Build the content course like I originally asked."*

**Model answer:** Don't argue from authority — offer a real test: pilot mentorship pairing with one cohort and the content-only plan with another, then compare quota-time data after one quarter. Naming a falsifiable test is more convincing than restating analysis.

---

### Module 4 — Mentor a Junior Designer's Draft
**Bloom level: Evaluate**
**Duty:** Mentor LXDs, coaching on solution design and architecture thinking

**Submitted frame:** A junior designer's storyboard opens with 4 bullet points defining "off-label use" before any scenario — accurate, but the learner hasn't encountered the moment yet.

**Build:**
1. What feedback do you give, and why — not just what to fix?
2. What's the underlying skill gap this reveals, beyond this one frame?
3. How do you deliver this without sounding like you're overriding their work?

---

### Module 5 — HDD From Scratch: Field Sales Scenario
**Bloom level: Create** *(full 6-step walkthrough inside one module)*
**Duty:** Author a complete High-Level Design, end to end, on a new scenario

**Brief:** A pharma client is launching a new injectable device. Field sales reps (avg. 4 years tenure) need to be ready to demo it within 6 weeks. Current state: a 90-minute live webinar exists but completion and confidence scores are mediocre, and reps report "information overload" with no chance to practice the physical demo motions.

**Six steps:**
1. **Remember** — List the 9 HDD section names from memory.
2. **Understand** — Restate the brief in your own words, 2-3 sentences.
3. **Apply** — Draft the Executive Summary (solution name, problem, architecture in one phrase, headline result if any).
4. **Analyze** — Break the brief into 3-4 distinct sub-problems (knowledge gap, practice gap, time-pressure gap, confidence gap). Don't solve yet.
5. **Evaluate** — For each sub-problem, choose and justify a modality (digital, live virtual, in-person/blended, job aid).
6. **Create** — Write the full Solution Architecture section: instructional model, modality table, one engagement mechanic — pulling from your Evaluate-step decisions.

---

### Module 6 — HDD From Scratch: Compliance Series
**Bloom level: Create** *(second rep, deliberately more ambiguous)*
**Duty:** Second full Create-level rep — different domain, same six-step discipline

**Brief:** Global compliance policy update rolling out across US, EU, and APAC field teams. Legal wants it done in 8 weeks. Previous compliance training had a 60% first-attempt pass rate and generated complaints about being "a checkbox exercise that doesn't reflect real situations."

**Six steps:**
1. **Remember** — List the 9 section names again, no peeking.
2. **Understand** — Restate the brief, naming the tension between the legal deadline and the "checkbox" complaint.
3. **Apply** — Draft the Business Context & Performance Gap section. (Hint: a 60% pass rate plus "checkbox" complaints points to a specific kind of failure, not just difficulty.)
4. **Analyze** — Separate what's genuinely region-specific from what's universal and shouldn't be rebuilt three times.
5. **Evaluate** — For one universal and one region-specific element, write the one-sentence justification you'd give a regional VP who wants to customize the universal one.
6. **Create** — Write Risks & Dependencies, including at least one genuinely uncomfortable risk (e.g. "legal's 8-week deadline may not allow real scenario-testing before launch").

---

### Module 7 — Executive Communication Rehearsal
**Bloom level: Evaluate**
**Duty:** Strong consultative, facilitation, and executive communication skills

**Setup:** A VP has 90 seconds and asks: *"Walk me through the device-launch training plan, and tell me why I should trust the timeline."*

**Model answer:** Lead with the gap, not the solution: "Reps currently get a 90-minute webinar and still feel unprepared because they've never practiced the physical demo motions before facing a real physician. The plan splits this into three pieces — self-paced knowledge content, a live coached practice session, and a quick-reference job aid for the visit itself. The 6-week timeline holds because the knowledge piece builds in parallel with the practice session design, and we're reusing the existing webinar content as raw material instead of starting from zero." Gap, architecture in one breath, timeline justification — under 90 seconds.

---

### Module 8 — Final Boss: Strategy-Level Gaps
**Bloom level: Evaluate**
**Duty:** 10-15% travel, executive-facing consulting capability, enterprise-level relationship building

**Real questions and honest answers:**

1. **"Tell me about a time you've mentored an LXD."** (You haven't, formally.)
   *"I haven't held a formal mentoring title yet, so I want to be upfront about that. What I can show you is the instinct for it — when I review a junior designer's storyboard frame, I focus feedback on the underlying design-thinking pattern, not just the one fix, which is the core of what mentoring actually is. I'd be building the formal experience, not the underlying skill."*

2. **"How do you talk about your current ceiling on executive-facing consulting?"**
   *"My stakeholder experience so far has been with university faculty and product teams, not C-suite executives at enterprise scale. The performance-consulting instinct — diagnosing the real gap before proposing a solution — is the same muscle, just exercised at a smaller scale."*

3. **"Do you see yourself as ready for LXA today, or growing into it?"**
   *"Growing into it, deliberately. I can author a complete HDD, diagnose a performance gap correctly, and defend a design decision with evidence — that's real. What I haven't done yet is operate at enterprise stakeholder scale or formally mentor a team. I'd rather be honest about that distinction than oversell it and have it show in the first quarter."*

---

## 6-Month Readiness Outcomes

**Learning Designer track:** A built Rise/Storyline portfolio piece, a documented blueprint-to-asset workflow, and rehearsed answers for the stakeholder-conflict and tools-gap questions — ready to demonstrate execution, not just describe it.

**LXA track:** Two authored HDDs from scratch (plus the AWJ retrospective as a third), a rehearsed performance-consulting diagnosis script, and practiced executive-level design-rationale communication — ready to be evaluated as a strategic partner, not just a builder.
